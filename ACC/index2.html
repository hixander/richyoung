Consulting services can include facility planning, design, renovation, construction administration, staff training, safety assessment and legal consultation and expert witness work.

Services

Facility Planning

Review programming and facility activities, compare fees, revenue and establish budget estimates. Provide recommendations for developing additional programming and help establish staff and budget needs.

Facility Evaluation and Support

Complete detailed facility evaluations including: demographic research, staff interviews, programming review, facility inspection and testing; all culminating in a report with prioritized recommendations, including budget estimates and color photos. The reports can include maintenance and operations, code compliance, programming, budget and staffing needs. Complete maintenance and Operation guidelines and reports.

Design Work

Complete research, design, plans and specifications useful for project architects, county health department approval, contractor bidding, construction and records. ACC is fully 2009 CAD compatible.

Geo-Thermal Work

Complete geothermal heat transfer and control systems. Including Stainless steel and titanium heat exchangers.

Construction Administration

Conduct pre-bid contractor walk-through, reviews of contractor submittals, conduct pre-construction meetings, inspections of contractor work for compliance with plans and specifications, reviews for progress payment requests, complete final inspections and review or draft as-built plans.

Product Testing and Evaluation

Provide “beta” testing for commercial pool equipment and systems. Complete data collection, evaluation and reports on equipment performance.

Risk Evaluation

Provide risk evaluation including OSHA Standards and Uniform Fire Code (Article 80) hazardous material handling compliance.

Legal Expert

Provide consultation and expert witness support on swimming pool and spa related issues.

About the Owner



Richard (Rich) Young is the principal consultant with over thirty years of public, institutional, commercial pool and water park experience. Rich pursued studies in Recreation Administration at San Diego State University. He continues to pursue educational opportunities to stay on the cutting edge of the industry.

Prior to 1983, as an aquatic director and manager, Rich operated several public and commercial facilities with both programming and maintenance responsibilities. Since then, he has worked as a sales, installation and service technician for pool mechanical systems and works as a mechanical equipment supplier and is a licensed contractor in several states. He has been an instructor for both the National Swimming Pool Foundation’s “Certified Pool Operator Course” since the early 1980s and has helped develop and teach the National Recreation and Park Association’s “Aquatic Facility Operator Course”. He is the managing editor of the 6th edition, NRPA, Aquatic Facility Operator Training Manual.

Rich often speaks at regional and national aquatic, swimming pool and recreation association conferences and has been published over thirty times in various pool industry periodicals and training texts on swimming pool, spa and water park water treatment, safety and construction issues.

Richard Young is a State of California licensed contractor with thirty years of experience. He is not a licensed engineer or architect. However, ACC has available for consultation: Pacific Engineering (electrical), Pool Engineer’s Inc. (structural), Kukuchi & Associates (landscape) and Patrell Engineering Works (mechanical) for special problem engineering and approval and sign-off on plans when appropriate or necessary. ACC has two administrative assistants and additional outside pool professionals to aid in both field and office work loads.
